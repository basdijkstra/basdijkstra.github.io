We test because we want to be informed about the state of the product we're working on, and about any potential problems that might occur when people use our product.

We use tools to support our testing (test automation) because we want valuable feedback, fast.

Being successful with test automation requires more than throwing a tool at a testing problem. That's where I come in.

I have close to two decades of experience in the field, and in those years, I have worked with a wide range of clients around the world, teaching them how to improve their test automation efforts.

I do this through training, consulting and mentoring, as well as through public speaking. No matter the form in which we work together, my approach is always tailor-made to fit your specific context and requirements.
